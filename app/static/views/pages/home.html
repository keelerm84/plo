Who we are

Pregnancy and Lactation associated Osteoporosis (PLO) as described by the National Osteoporosis Society in the UK is, ‘a very unusual situation when bones break suddenly or severe pain occurs during or soon after pregnancy in otherwise apparently healthy women.’ In their leaflet describing this condition, they go on to explain that,’ Osteoporosis associated with pregnancy is a rare condition when bones break (fracture) easily, usually in the spine, or occasionally the hip, around the time a woman is giving birth, causing pain and disability.’

This website has been created by a group of women from all over the world who suffer from this condition. We have found that because this condition is so rare, there are not many resources available to support women who are suffering. We hope that this website can address this need. A common experience for the women in our group was intense back pain shortly after or just before giving birth to our babies. The pain was often so intense that it affected our ability to lift our babies and generally care for our babies in the way that is expected of new mothers.

Please take some time to explore our website and what it has to offer including testimonials from women who have experienced PLO, tips for living with PLO, Frequently Asked Questions and resources to build your understanding of this condition.

You can access the full information leaflet quoted above as well as many other useful resources by clicking on our resource page.
