Melissa Richardson

Six months in to my first pregnancy and I felt a sudden pain in my groin as I stepped off the curb on a daily walk. The pain worsened and eventually I could not bear my own body weight. No one could explain what was happening, so I was in pain and frightened as labour approached. I gave birth naturally to a beautiful boy, but was in so much pain I could hardly bear to look at him. It was only after I left the hospital and had an x ray that it was discovered that I had osteoporosis and multiple fractures in the top of my left femur. This was 25 years ago.

I found a doctor who was interested enough to find out more and the diagnosis was transient regional osteoporosis of pregnancy. Three months later I was able to walk again and within 12 months my bone density had returned to normal and I was completely pain free. Five years later I had a second child. My bone density was monitored, and when the levels reduced, I gave myself daily injections of calcitonin until the baby was born. I had a very uneventful labour and no fractures! Alex (25) and Louis (20) light up my life. I would do it all again if I had to.
